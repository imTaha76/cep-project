Types of Soil

  • Loamy: The gardener's best friend. Rich, fertile, and well-draining.
  • Sandy: Gritty texture. Drains quickly, often needs nutrients added.
  • Clayey: Sticky and dense. Holds water well but can become compacted.
  • Black Soil: Rich in minerals and humus, excellent for crops like cotton.
  • Alluvial: Deposited by rivers. Highly fertile and found in plains.

Protection Practices

  • Cover Cropping: Plant crops to cover soil between main harvests.
  • Contour Farming: Plow along the contours of the land to prevent runoff.
  • Composting: Enrich soil naturally with organic waste.
  • Crop Rotation: Prevent nutrient depletion by changing crops seasonally.
